《sonar flare ii》

My initial plan for this performance was to use a recording from a funeral where I read "Crossing the Bar", by Alfred Tennyson. I wanted to focus on the theme of transition, but the choir recording I had been using was challenging to make musical at every pitch with the sampler I was using. Instead, I wanted to try and move away from using music samples altogether and see what I could create with a similar setup as my first performance but with no music. The night before my second performance, I scrapped the set that I had practiced for weeks and started over.

I took a recording of a bridge over the Mississippi River in Memphis taken before the funeral and mixed it with a recording of a dawn chorus in Chatsworth Park taken after the funeral. It was processed live with a Morphagene (Make Noise), Stages (Mutable Instruments), a Cold Mac (Mannequins), and a 2hp comb filter.

Sonar Flare II was a follow-up event in August 2022 curated and run by Cy Kim where I was invited to play at a second time. This time, it took place at the original warehouse location in East Williamsburg called Hex House. My set was accompanied by live visuals from Armon Naeini.
