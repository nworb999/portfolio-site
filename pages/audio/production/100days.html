《100 days of sound design》

After seeing my friends do a 100 days challenge for their graduate program, I was inspired to do the same. I wanted to force myself to work with sound every day, even if it meant lightly EQing a field recording. I worked from Winter 2021 to Spring 2022.

Naturally, the main obstacle was the fear of making bad stuff. Forcing myself to upload early and often had been an important part of my practice in order to overcome that anxiety, but this project was especially challenging because I uploaded the result of each session, no matter how unhappy I was with it. It helped me take myself less seriously and see each work as part of an ongoing progression.

I primarily used Ableton Live along with my Moog DFAM, Pocket Operator Sub, and the Make Noise Morphagene. I'm generally more comfortable working with only hardware so I pushed myself to use the DAW for most tracks. I also experimented with story-telling by mixing field recordings and synth samples together.

In hindsight, while I learned a lot from maintaining a daily practice, I found that forcing myself to post a result every day caused me to cut corners instead of try new things, especially given the small window of time after my day-job that I had to work.
