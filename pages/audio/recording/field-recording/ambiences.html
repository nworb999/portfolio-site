    • 《ambiences》

    I began recording things during the pandemic after I started micing up my guqin for online lessons with a borrowed microphone. I didn't know anything about best practices while recording acoustic instruments or anything about EQing and post-production. The first thing I noticed was how much more accurately I could hear the slide notes with my guqin. The second thing I noticed was how loud the sounds of my shared apartment were.

    I began to embrace the honesty and imperfections of my early recordings, including my frustrating guqin practice, the sounds of my flatmates in the apartment, as well as the atmosphere of the neighborhood outside. Eventually, I began to capture more recordings outside to capture the unique ambiances of the surrounding areas. The early pandemic completely transformed the soundscape of my neighborhood, and I began to appreciate the significance of formerly mundane sounds like the bustle of my street.

    With a focus on the historical relevance of every sound in the context of its location, I continue to explore the everyday noises that mark the distinct character of a place. I categorize and number each of my recordings, leaving room for the possibility of recapturing that location in the future.
