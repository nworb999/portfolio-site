《guqin》

The guqin is a seven-stringed zither instrument that is over 3,000 years old. I’ve been studying guqin since 2019 with Weeteng Soh’s Cool Fragrance Qin Loft, based out of Singapore. She is of the Guangling Pai lineage of players, originating from the Jiangnan area of China. Since the pandemic, we have done early morning video lessons. Because of the 12-hour time difference, our lessons begin at 7am or 8am, depending on daylight savings.

I first discovered the instrument after attending an introductory class I was invited to by a friend while working in Singapore, and I immediately fell in love with the quiet, bassy tone of the instrument. Attending that intro class was fateful in many ways, but most of all it initiated my (re)introduction to working with sound.

There are many challenges that come with learning only through video, and most of the resources for guqin are not yet translated to English. I try to record regularly to capture improvement.
